## Introduction

I'm thrilled to announce that I've successfully completed the **Security Manpower Training Program (SMTP) 2025** organized by the Korea Information Security Industry Association (KISIA), earning a **High Distinction** for my performance throughout the intensive 36-hour training program. This fully-funded program by the South Korean government provided an exceptional opportunity to learn from top Korean cybersecurity professionals.

"This Program is very strict"

## About SMTP 2025

The Security Manpower Training (SMT) Program is a prestigious, high-quality information security education initiative commissioned by KISIA and NSHC Inc., a leading Korean cybersecurity company. The program aims to educate and secure a pool of cybersecurity professionals through specialized and systematic training courses.

### Program Highlights

- **Duration**: July 21 - August 14, 2025 (36 hours)
- **Format**: 100% online training with Korean cybersecurity experts
- **Cost**: Completely free and fully funded by the South Korean government
- **Platform**: [SecurityGround.net](https://securityground.net)
- **Certificate ID**: SMTP2025-029FDA

> This program brought together **329 participants** from **10+ prestigious universities** across Indonesia, creating a vibrant learning community.
{: .prompt-info }

## Comprehensive Curriculum

The training was structured into a perfect blend of self-study, live lectures, practical assessments, and expert sessions across six core cybersecurity domains:

### Week 1: Web & Network Security Foundation

**Web Security**
- Understanding common web vulnerabilities
- OWASP Top 10 security risks
- Hands-on challenges with real-world scenarios
- Practical exploitation and mitigation techniques

**Network Security**
- Network protocols and security fundamentals
- Traffic analysis and packet inspection
- Network attack vectors and defense mechanisms
- Security tools and monitoring techniques

The week concluded with an expert lecture from industry professionals, providing insights into current cybersecurity trends and challenges.

### Week 2: Operating Systems & Malware Analysis

**Linux & Windows OS Security**
- System hardening techniques
- User and permission management
- Security configurations and best practices
- OS-specific vulnerability assessments

**Malware Analysis**
- Understanding malware types and behaviors
- Static and dynamic analysis techniques
- Ransomware trends and mitigation
- Reverse engineering fundamentals

A **Mid-Assessment** tested our knowledge on Web Security, Network Security, and OS Security, ensuring solid understanding before moving forward.

### Week 3: Cryptography & Intelligence Gathering

**Cryptography Fundamentals**
- Encryption and decryption principles
- Hash functions and digital signatures
- SSL/TLS and secure communications
- Cryptographic attack methods

**Open Source Intelligence (OSINT)**
- Information gathering methodologies
- Social media intelligence
- Digital footprint analysis
- OSINT tools and frameworks
- Two special lectures diving deep into advanced OSINT techniques

### Week 4: Final Assessment & Expert Insights

The final week featured:
- Comprehensive final assessment covering all six topics
- Final expert lecture on industry best practices
- Reflection and knowledge consolidation

## Learning Approach & Assessment

The program employed a robust grading system with multiple evaluation components:

| Component | Points | Description |
|:----------|:------:|:------------|
| Attendance | 120 | 12 online sessions (10 points each) |
| Quizzes | 120 | 6 topic quizzes (20 points each) |
| Challenges | 180 | 6 practical challenges (30 points each) |
| Mid Assessment | 160 | Topics 1-3 comprehensive test |
| Final Assessment | 200 | All topics comprehensive test |
| Journals | 220 | Expert lecture reflections (5 journals) |
| **Total** | **1000** | **Complete grading scheme** |
